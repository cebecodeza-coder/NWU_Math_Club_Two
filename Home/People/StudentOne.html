BEng Mechanical Engineering

Michelle Williams

by Webber Productions

Michelle Williams doesn’t announce herself loudly. She doesn’t need to. The evidence is in the work. A second-year mechanical engineering student, her path was shaped early at Pretoria High School for Girls, where a sharp instinct for mathematics quickly turned into something more deliberate.

Under the guidance of a teacher who recognized that spark, she moved beyond the syllabus—taking AP Mathematics, entering Olympiads, and treating each problem less as an answer to find and more as something to understand.

There was a moment, somewhere between curiosity and discipline, when that mindset became unmistakable. She derived the volume of a sphere on her own—not for marks, not for recognition, but because she wanted to see if she could.

That quiet determination carried forward. Engineering, for Williams, is not a departure from mathematics but its extension: a way to translate abstract reasoning into structure, motion, and design.
